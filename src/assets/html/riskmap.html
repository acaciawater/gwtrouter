A project location can be selected by clicking a location on the map. If a project involves multiple locations, choose one of these to start with. At the end of the assessment, the project location can be changed to gain insight into how the potential impacts on groundwater resources vary between sites.

 

The map shows the well-known Aqueduct Water Risk Atlas overall water risk map. The risk map is based on a range of indicators relevant to water quantity, water quality, and regulatory and reputational risks. Though the scope of the Aqueduct tool is broader than that of the Groundwater Impact Scan, it provides a first impression of water risk globally, and in the project area.

 

Reference:

Gassert, F., Landis, M., Luck, M., Reig, P., & Shiao, T. (2013). “Aqueduct global maps 2.0.” Working Paper. Washington, DC: World Resources Institure. Available online at /publication/aqueduct-global-maps-20.
